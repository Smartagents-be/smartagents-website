# SmartAgents blog style guide

Use this guide whenever writing or editing a SmartAgents blog post. Apply every rule below without exception.

---

## Voice and tone

- **Direct and grounded.** Write like a practitioner talking to another business person, not like a marketer or a tech journalist. No hype.
- **We, not I.** SmartAgents speaks as a team. Use "we", "ons", "onze".
- **Short sentences win.** One idea per sentence. A short sentence after a longer one creates rhythm — use that pattern deliberately.
- **Concrete over abstract.** Name the thing. "AI agents die je offertes verwerken" beats "AI-oplossingen voor uw bedrijfsprocessen."
- **Conversational but not casual.** No slang. No filler. No "uiteraard" or "zeker en vast."

---

## Structure and cadence

- **Open with a tension or observation**, not a definition. The reader should recognise something from their own experience in the first two sentences.
- **One idea per paragraph.** Three to four sentences max. White space is part of the rhythm.
- **Use h2 for sections**, h3 only if a section needs a genuine sub-division. Headings are short statements or questions, not chapter titles.
- **Blockquotes are for a single sharp line** that earns its own pause. One per post, used sparingly.
- **Bullet lists only when items are truly parallel and enumerable.** Never use a list as a substitute for a paragraph that would flow naturally.
- **End with a clear action or forward motion.** A call to contact, a next step, a commitment. Never a vague "we kijken ernaar uit."

---

## Punctuation and typography rules

- **No em-dash (— or --).** Replace with a colon, a comma, a full stop, or restructure the sentence entirely. This is a hard rule.
- **No en-dash as a separator.** Use a colon or rephrase.
- **Quotation marks** for direct speech or a named concept the first time it appears.
- **Bold** for product names (SmartSpace, SmartScan) and for the one phrase per paragraph that carries the most weight. Do not bold more than one phrase per paragraph.
- **No exclamation marks.**

---

## Language specifics (Dutch posts)

- Uppercase "AI" and "AI agents" — consistent with existing posts.
- Product names always capitalised: SmartAgents, SmartSpace, SmartScan.
- Avoid anglicisms when a plain Dutch word exists. "Doorlichting" not "assessment." "Toepassingen" not "applicaties" (unless quoting a UI term).
- Use "jij/jouw" (informal you) throughout — never "u/uw."

---

## Language specifics (English posts)

- British spelling: "organisation", "optimise", "behaviour."
- Same informality: "you/your", never "one."
- Translate intent, not words. A Dutch idiom should become a natural English equivalent.

---

## Frontmatter checklist

Every post must have:
- `title` — sentence case, no trailing punctuation, no em-dash
- `date` — YYYY-MM-DD
- `categories` — one or two slugs from `_data/blogCategories.js`
- `excerpt` — one or two sentences, same voice as the post, no em-dash
- `image` — path under `/assets/blog/`
- `imageAlt` — descriptive, no em-dash
- `imagePosition` — only if the focal point is not centre (e.g. `left center`)

---

## What to avoid

- Em-dash in any form (—, --)
- Rhetorical questions as section openers
- Phrases like "in deze blog", "zoals eerder vermeld", "ten slotte"
- Numbered takeaways or "key insights" sections
- Summarising what you just said at the end of a section
